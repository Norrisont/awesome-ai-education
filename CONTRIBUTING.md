# How to Contribute to Awesome AI for Education

Thank you for helping make this list better!

---

## How to add a resource

1. **Fork this repo** — click Fork top right on GitHub
2. **Edit README.md** — add your resource in this format:

```markdown
- **[Tool Name](https://verified-link.com)** — One sentence on what it does.
  > *Why it matters: One sentence on real-world US education impact.*
```

3. **Open a Pull Request** — title: `Add [Tool Name] to [Section Name]`

---

## Rules

- ✅ Verified working link
- ✅ Free, open-source, or meaningful free tier
- ✅ US education relevant (FERPA, K-12, higher ed, community college)
- ✅ Plain English — no jargon
- ✅ Actively maintained (last update within 2 years)
- ❌ No fully paid tools
- ❌ No broken links
- ❌ No self-promotion without `[Disclosure: I built this]`

---

## Writing Good Descriptions

| Good | Bad |
|---|---|
| "Free AI tutor used by 150M students worldwide" | "ML-powered adaptive pedagogical inference platform" |
| "Works offline — no internet needed" | "Edge-deployed low-latency content delivery system" |
| "Free for all verified US K-12 teachers" | "Freemium SaaS with educator tier discounts" |

---

## Fixing Broken Links

Fork the repo, fix the URL or remove the entry, then open a PR titled: `Fix broken link: Tool Name`

---

## Suggesting New Sections

Open an **Issue** (not a PR), describe the section you want, and list 3–5 example resources you'd include.

---

## Code of Conduct

Respectful and constructive conversation only. No discrimination of any kind.
