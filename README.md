## What is this?

AI is reshaping education — but resources are scattered, expensive, or require fast internet that many students don't have.

**This list is different.** Everything here is:

- ✅ Free, open-source, or has a meaningful free tier
- ✅ Working and actively maintained (verified April 2026)
- ✅ Written in plain English — no jargon
- ✅ US-market focused (FERPA-compliant, K-12 through higher ed)
- ✅ Especially useful for underserved students, Title I schools, and community colleges

## 🤖 AI Tutors & Learning Assistants

> AI that helps students understand concepts, get unstuck, and learn at their own pace — available 24/7.

- **[Khan Academy Khanmigo](https://www.khanacademy.org/khan-labs)** — Free AI tutor built on GPT-4. Explains concepts step-by-step. Asks Socratic questions instead of just giving answers. Available to all US students for free.
  > *A student in an underfunded school in Wilmington, DE gets the same quality tutoring as one in a private school in NYC.*

- **[Socratic by Google](https://socratic.org/)** — Free app (iOS and Android). Take a photo of any homework question and get a step-by-step explanation. Works for math, science, history, literature, and more.
  > *No account needed. Just point your camera at a problem.*

- **[ChatGPT for Teachers](https://openai.com/chatgpt/education/)** — Free for all verified US K-12 educators through June 2027. Includes GPT-4, file uploads, image generation, and FERPA-compliant workspace.

- **[Duolingo](https://www.duolingo.com/)** — Free AI-powered language learning. Adapts to each student's pace. Available for 40+ languages. Used by millions of US immigrant students and ELL learners.

- **[Carnegie Learning MATHia](https://www.carnegielearning.com/solutions/math/mathia/)** — AI math tutor with the strongest research base in the industry. RCT-validated. Free access program available for Title I schools.
  > *The most studied AI tutoring tool in K-12. The research is solid.*

- **[Synthesis](https://www.synthesis.com/)** — AI-powered problem-solving for K-12 students. Originally built for SpaceX employees' kids. Significant free access available.

## 👩‍🏫 Tools for K-12 Teachers

> AI that saves teachers time so they can focus on students, not paperwork.

### Lesson Planning & Content Creation

- **[MagicSchool AI](https://www.magicschool.ai/)** — Free AI platform used by 3+ million US teachers. Generates lesson plans, rubrics, differentiated materials, parent emails, IEP accommodations, and 60+ other tools.
  > *The most widely adopted free AI tool in US K-12. Teachers report saving 5+ hours per week.*

- **[Diffit](https://web.diffit.me/)** — Free. Paste any topic, article, or YouTube video and instantly get reading materials adapted to different grade levels and Lexile scores. FERPA-compliant.
  > *Perfect for teachers with mixed-ability classrooms or ELL students.*

- **[Brisk Teaching](https://www.briskteaching.com/)** — Free Chrome extension that adds AI to Google Docs, Slides, and Forms. 93% Common Sense Privacy Rating. Used by thousands of US districts.

- **[Canva for Education](https://www.canva.com/education/)** — Free for verified K-12 teachers and students. AI tools for creating visual lesson content, presentations, and worksheets. COPPA and FERPA compliant.

- **[Google Gemini for Education](https://edu.google.com/ai/gemini-for-education/)** — Free for all Google Workspace for Education users. Integrated into Docs, Slides, Forms, and Google Classroom. Announced at ISTE 2025.

### Classroom Communication

- **[ClassDojo](https://www.classdojo.com/)** — Free. The most widely used classroom communication app in the US. AI-generated weekly progress summaries for parents. COPPA-compliant.

- **[Remind](https://www.remind.com/)** — Free messaging between teachers, students, and parents. AI translation for messages in 70+ languages. Works via SMS — no smartphone required for parents.
  > *Critical for reaching parents in low-income communities who may not have smartphones.*

- **[Bloomz](https://www.bloomz.com/)** — Free classroom management and parent communication. Auto-translates messages into 130+ languages. Used across US districts with large ELL populations.

### Grading & Assessment

- **[Gradescope](https://www.gradescope.com/)** — AI-assisted grading. Groups similar student answers so teachers grade one batch at a time. Free for individual teachers (Gradescope Basic).

- **[Turnitin](https://www.turnitin.com/)** — Plagiarism and AI-detection. Available through most US schools and universities institutionally.

- **[Quill.org](https://www.quill.org/)** — Completely free AI writing feedback for grades 1-12. Gives students instant, specific feedback on grammar and writing before the teacher grades it. Used by 5+ million US students.

## 🎓 Higher Education & College Tools

> AI for US colleges, universities, and community colleges.

- **[OpenStax](https://openstax.org/)** — Peer-reviewed, free college textbooks for 50+ courses. Used by 7+ million US students annually. API available for EdTech developers.
  > *A community college student saves $200+ per course by using OpenStax instead of a commercial textbook.*

- **[MIT OpenCourseWare](https://ocw.mit.edu/)** — Free access to all MIT course materials — syllabi, lecture notes, problem sets, exams. No enrollment, no account needed.

- **[Coursera (Audit Mode)](https://www.coursera.org/)** — Most courses can be audited for free. Includes AI and ML courses from top US universities.

- **[edX (Audit Mode)](https://www.edx.org/)** — Free access to courses from Harvard, MIT, and 160+ other institutions. Professional certificates available (paid).

- **[JSTOR Access for Researchers](https://www.jstor.org/)** — Free access to 100+ journals for qualifying institutions and independent researchers.

- **[Google Colab](https://colab.research.google.com/)** — Free cloud Jupyter notebooks with GPU access. Used by US universities for AI and data science courses. No setup required.

- **[GitHub Student Developer Pack](https://education.github.com/pack)** — Free access to 100+ developer tools for verified US students. Includes GitHub Copilot, cloud credits, and more.

## 🎓 Mentorship & Student Support

> AI tools for connecting students with mentors and providing personalized academic guidance.

- **[MentroAI](https://mentroai.com)** — Platform for colleges and universities to run structured mentorship programs. Program manager dashboard, mentor/mentee mobile apps, and progress tracking in one system. [Disclosure: I built this]
  > *Designed specifically for the US higher ed market. Tracks mentorship program ROI for administrators.*

- **[College Advising Corps](https://www.advisingcorps.org/)** — Places recent college graduates in under-resourced US high schools as college advisers. Technology platform available to partner schools.

- **[Peer 2 Peer University (P2PU)](https://www.p2pu.org/)** — Free learning circles. Groups meet in US public libraries to learn together using free online courses. No facilitator expertise needed.

- **[NACADA AI Resources](https://www.nacada.ksu.edu/)** — National academic advising association. Free resources on using AI in college advising workflows.

- **[AdmitHub (Now Mainstay)](https://www.mainstay.com/)** — AI chatbot for student engagement and retention. Used by US community colleges to reduce summer melt and improve first-year retention. Free pilots available.
  > *Texted 18,000 incoming community college students. Increased enrollment by 3.9 percentage points.*

## 📝 Assessment & Feedback AI

> Tools for measuring learning and giving students useful, timely feedback.

- **[Formative](https://www.formative.com/)** — Free real-time assessment platform. Teachers see student responses live as they type. FERPA-compliant. Used across US K-12 districts.

- **[Edulastic](https://edulastic.com/)** — Free AI-scored assessments aligned to Common Core and state standards. Instant analytics for teachers.

- **[Quill.org](https://www.quill.org/)** — (Also in Teacher Tools.) Free AI writing feedback platform. 5+ million US students. Aligned to Common Core ELA standards.

- **[ASSISTments](https://www.assistments.org/)** — Free math homework help with immediate feedback. Research-backed. Used by US middle and high school students. NSF-funded.
  > *Students get hints when stuck. Teachers get data on exactly where students are struggling.*

- **[Nearpod](https://nearpod.com/)** — Free tier. Interactive lessons with real-time assessment. Students answer on their devices. Teachers see responses instantly. Works with Google Classroom.

- **[Pear Deck](https://www.peardeck.com/)** — Free Google Slides and PowerPoint add-on. Add interactive questions to any presentation. Students respond in real time.

## 🌐 Multilingual & ELL Support

> AI tools for the 5+ million English Language Learners in US public schools.

*10% of US public school students are English Language Learners (ELLs). Most AI tools are English-only. These aren't.*

- **[Duolingo for Schools](https://schools.duolingo.com/)** — Free teacher dashboard. Assign Duolingo lessons and track student progress. 40+ languages. COPPA-compliant.

- **[Google Translate](https://translate.google.com/)** — Free. 133 languages. Camera translation for textbooks. Real-time conversation mode. API available for developers: 500,000 free characters/month.

- **[Sora (OverDrive) Accessible Books](https://www.overdrive.com/apps/sora/)** — Free ebook and audiobook app for US K-12 students (requires school to have OverDrive). Includes books in Spanish, Chinese, Arabic, and more.

- **[Newsela ELL](https://newsela.com/)** — News articles adapted to 5 reading levels in English and Spanish. Free tier available. Widely used in US Title I schools.

- **[Remind](https://www.remind.com/)** — (Also in Teacher Tools.) Translates teacher-parent messages into 70+ languages automatically. Critical for communicating with ELL families.

- **[Flipgrid (Microsoft)](https://info.flip.com/)** — Free video discussion tool. ELL students can express ideas verbally without the pressure of writing. FERPA-compliant. COPPA-compliant.

- **[Whisper (OpenAI)](https://github.com/openai/whisper)** — Free, open-source speech recognition in 90+ languages. Self-hostable. Build voice-based tools for ELL students who struggle with writing.

## 📡 Offline & Low-Bandwidth Tools

> Tools that work without reliable internet — important for rural US schools and low-income students.

*21 million Americans — including 9 million children — lack home broadband access. These tools bridge the homework gap.*

- **[Kolibri by Learning Equality](https://learningequality.org/kolibri/)** — Free offline learning platform. Contains Khan Academy content, textbooks, and exercises. Runs on a basic laptop or Raspberry Pi. Used by US schools in low-connectivity areas.
  > *The single best tool for students without home internet. Give them Kolibri on a cheap device.*

- **[Wikipedia Offline (Kiwix)](https://www.kiwix.org/)** — Download Wikipedia, Project Gutenberg, Khan Academy, and more for offline access. Free. Works on any device.
  > *Entire English Wikipedia = ~21GB. Fits on a $10 USB drive. Gives students access to millions of articles without internet.*

- **[Rachel (Remote Area Community Hotspot for Education)](https://worldpossible.org/)** — A small device that stores educational content and serves it as a local WiFi network. Students connect with any device. Used in US schools in rural areas.

- **[Endless OS](https://www.endlessos.org/)** — Free operating system pre-loaded with educational apps and Wikipedia. Designed for low-connectivity environments. Used by US school districts.

- **[FCC E-Rate Program](https://www.fcc.gov/consumers/guides/universal-service-program-schools-and-libraries-e-rate)** — US government program that subsidizes internet access for schools and libraries. Not a tool, but critical for EdTech builders to know about.
  > *E-Rate funds 20-90% of internet costs for qualifying US schools. Know this exists when building for Title I schools.*

## 🧰 Developer Tools for EdTech

> The technical building blocks for EdTech products in the US market.

### AI APIs & Models

- **[OpenAI API](https://platform.openai.com/)** — Powers Khanmigo, ChatGPT for Teachers, and thousands of EdTech tools. Free trial credits. Pay per use. Education pricing available.

- **[Anthropic Claude API](https://www.anthropic.com/api)** — Strong at tutoring, explanation, and safe interactions with students. Good for educational contexts where safety and age-appropriateness matter.

- **[Google Gemini API](https://ai.google.dev/)** — Free tier: 1,500 requests/day. Multimodal (processes images and text). Useful for homework photo tools.

- **[HuggingFace](https://huggingface.co/)** — Free access to thousands of open-source AI models. Free inference API for small projects. Host your own models.

### Learning Management Systems (LMS)

- **[Moodle](https://moodle.org/)** — The most widely used open-source LMS worldwide. Free. Used by US community colleges and K-12 districts. 300+ million users.

- **[Open edX](https://openedx.org/)** — Open-source platform behind edX. Free to self-host. Powers online courses for millions of students. Used by US universities.

- **[Canvas LMS (Free for Teachers)](https://www.instructure.com/canvas)** — The most widely used LMS in US higher education. Free version available for individual educators.

- **[Google Classroom](https://classroom.google.com/)** — Free for all US schools with Google Workspace for Education. Integrates with Google Docs, Slides, Forms, and now Gemini.

### Learning Analytics & Standards

- **[xAPI (Tin Can API)](https://xapi.com/)** — Open standard for tracking learning data across platforms. Use it to build dashboards that aggregate student progress from multiple tools.

- **[IMS Global Standards](https://www.imsglobal.org/)** — Open standards organization for EdTech interoperability. LTI (Learning Tools Interoperability) lets your tool plug into any US LMS.

- **[CASE (Curriculum and Standards Exchange)](https://www.imsglobal.org/case)** — Open standard for sharing curriculum standards data (Common Core, NGSS, state standards). Build alignment features into your EdTech product.

## 🔒 FERPA, Privacy & Responsible AI in Education

> Building EdTech in the US requires FERPA compliance for student data privacy.

*FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act) protects student education records. COPPA applies to children under 13. Both are federal law.*

### Privacy Compliance Resources

- **[Student Privacy Compass (PTAC)](https://studentprivacy.ed.gov/)** — Free resource from the US Department of Education. Guides for FERPA compliance, vendor contracts, and student data privacy policies.
  > *Before deploying any AI tool in a US school, consult this resource.*

- **[Common Sense Media Privacy Ratings](https://www.commonsense.org/education/privacy/ratings)** — Free ratings of EdTech tools' privacy practices. Schools and parents use these to evaluate tools. Getting a high rating is a major trust signal.

- **[CoSN (Consortium for School Networking)](https://www.cosn.org/)** — US school technology leaders network. Free resources on AI governance, data privacy, and responsible EdTech procurement.

- **[ISTE AI in Education Ethics Guide](https://iste.org/ai)** — Free guidelines from ISTE for responsibly implementing AI in K-12 classrooms. Widely referenced by US districts.

### AI Fairness in Education

- **[AI Fairness 360 (IBM)](https://aif360.mybluemix.net/)** — Free toolkit to detect and fix bias in AI models. Important for ensuring adaptive learning tools don't disadvantage certain student groups.

- **[Algorithmic Justice League](https://www.ajl.org/)** — Advocacy organization focused on AI bias. Free research and resources on bias in educational AI.

- **[US Department of Education: Artificial Intelligence and the Future of Teaching and Learning](https://www2.ed.gov/documents/ai-report/ai-report.pdf)** — Free PDF. The official US government guidance on responsible AI in education.

## 📖 Research & US Policy Reports

> Key papers and US government reports — with plain-English summaries.

### Key Research Papers

- **[Intelligent Tutoring Systems Survey (2020)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.12744)** — Comprehensive overview of how AI tutors work and what the evidence says.
  > *Plain English: One-on-one AI tutoring can be as effective as human tutoring — one of the strongest findings in education research.*

- **[Bridging the Gap: AI for Underserved Learners (2024)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/he.20511)** — How AI tools can close gaps for low-income, rural, and first-generation students.
  > *Plain English: AI can give Title I students access to support that used to only exist at expensive private schools.*

- **[Deep Knowledge Tracing (Piech et al., 2015)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05908)** — How deep learning can predict what a student knows and what they should learn next. Foundation of adaptive learning systems.

- **[Two Sigma Problem: Building a Master Tutor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloom%27s_2_sigma_problem)** — Bloom's seminal finding that one-on-one tutoring improves student performance by 2 standard deviations. Why AI tutors matter.

### US Government Reports

- **[US Dept of Education: AI and the Future of Teaching and Learning (2023)](https://www2.ed.gov/documents/ai-report/ai-report.pdf)** — Free PDF. Official US guidance on AI in schools. Required reading for US EdTech builders.

- **[NCES Condition of Education 2024](https://nces.ed.gov/programs/coe/)** — Free annual report on the state of US education. Key statistics on achievement gaps, access, and equity.

- **[FTC Report: Protecting Kids Online (2024)](https://www.ftc.gov/reports/protecting-kids-online)** — US Federal Trade Commission report on children's data privacy and EdTech. Important for COPPA compliance.

- **[ISTE: AI in Education Policy Toolkit (2024)](https://iste.org/ai)** — Free toolkit for school districts developing AI policies. Widely adopted by US school boards.

## 💡 Project Ideas

> Real problems in US education that need builders. Pick one.

### Beginner Friendly

- [ ] **Title I school AI readiness dashboard** — Pull NCES Common Core data + CRDC data to map which US school districts have the highest need and lowest AI tool adoption. Visualize it for policymakers.

- [ ] **FERPA-compliant teacher email drafter** — MagicSchool AI-style tool that generates parent communication emails from a few bullet points. No student data stored.

- [ ] **Common Core standard aligner** — Input any lesson plan → AI maps it to relevant Common Core or state standards → generates alignment report for the teacher.

- [ ] **College Scorecard explorer** — Build a conversational interface on top of the US College Scorecard API. Students ask "which colleges near me have the highest graduation rates for low-income students?" and get real data.

### Intermediate

- [ ] **Summer melt prevention bot** — Use Twilio + AI to text newly graduated high school seniors over the summer to keep them engaged with their college enrollment process. (Based on the AdmitHub research.)

- [ ] **Adaptive ELL vocabulary builder** — Build a Duolingo-style vocabulary app using Common Voice data for pronunciation + NAEP word lists. Targets English learners at specific grade levels.

- [ ] **First-generation college student advisor** — AI chatbot trained on FAFSA, Common App, and financial aid data. Helps first-gen students navigate the college application process step-by-step.

- [ ] **IEP accommodations generator** — Teacher inputs student's diagnosis and grade level → AI suggests evidence-based IEP accommodations aligned to IDEA standards.

### Advanced

- [ ] **Adaptive learning engine for US curriculum** — Implement Knowledge Tracing on ASSISTments dataset to predict student mastery of specific Common Core math standards, then recommend next practice problems.

- [ ] **Achievement gap early warning system** — Use Stanford SEDA + CRDC data to train a model predicting which school districts will see the largest achievement gaps 3 years from now.

- [ ] **Federated student performance model** — Use PySyft to train a shared reading growth prediction model across 5 school districts without sharing any student records.

## 🙌 How to Contribute

This list stays useful because contributors like you keep it updated.

### Adding a Resource

1. **Fork this repo** — click "Fork" in the top right on GitHub
2. **Edit `README.md`** — find the right section and add your resource:

```markdown
- **[Tool Name](https://verified-working-link.com)** — One sentence on what it does.
  > *Why it matters: One sentence on the real-world impact for US students or educators.*
```

3. **Open a Pull Request** — include a one-line description of what you added and why

### Contribution Rules

- ✅ Link must be verified working — test it before submitting
- ✅ Must be free, open-source, or have a meaningful free tier
- ✅ Must be relevant to US education (FERPA, K-12, higher ed, or community college)
- ✅ Plain English only — no unexplained acronyms or jargon
- ✅ Actively maintained (last update within 2 years)
- ❌ No fully paid tools without a meaningful free option
- ❌ No broken or unverified links
- ❌ No self-promotion without `[Disclosure: I built this]`

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for full details.
